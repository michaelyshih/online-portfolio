About me

My name is Michael. I am a software engineer who initially found interest in software and technologies in highschool through Computer Science classes. While I was working in my first job as a sales assistant, I had the opportunity to apply the skills I’ve learned in high school to create an excel data parser that transferred data from software into excel. When a friend suggested an opportunity to learn more about software engineering, I jumped right on board. Prior to entering

App Academy, a highly - competitive, software - engineering bootcamp, I was enthusiastic about the program and proved to be a fast learner. I was able to complete 4 weeks worth of materials in 4 days. While I was with the academy, I was able to build a full-stack clone of the e-commerce site, skull candy. In 2 weeks, I finished the project using Ruby on Rails, postgresQL, and React/ Redux. I was also able to cooperate with other engineers to create a complete, full-stack, food-sharing MERN project, using express, mongoDB, and Nodejs in a week as well.

I believe my high adaptability in learning and my prior experiences in a fast paced environment will prove to be a great asset for any company.

Projects

Projects

Saikyou

An interactive way of viewing anime like a gallery. It display the top 3 popular anime, manga, anime music of each year for the last 5 years. Allows you to scroll through a timeline in 3d space exploring the most popular anime / manga/ music of that year. You could select year by scrolling slider and when clicked, the item you picked will display the information of the anime with links and details to external sites where you could explore more.

SoulCandy

Soulcandy is a full stack clone of Skullcandy, an e-commerce website specifically for headphones. It allows users to view and purchase products. Users could save their cart, which will only be removed on proactive logout. It was built with a React Redux frontend and a Ruby on Rails backend. It features simple library such as Swiper.js to help browse the selected item with ease.

Yum

When you cook at home for one, you’re always buying too much and have limited options in what to make. Yum solves this problem by allowing users to host events and share their excess cooking. Users can not only cut down on food costs, but also share the joy of food with others! Users can also be guests of events, by which they can enjoy home cooked meals from verified chefs/ hosts at a fraction of the normal cost they may pay for a meal.
